# E-commerce Sales Analysis using SQL

## Project Overview

This project analyzes an e-commerce dataset to uncover insights related to customer behavior, traffic sources, marketing campaigns, product performance, customer retention, and revenue generation.

## Dataset

The dataset contains information about:

- Website sessions
- Orders
- Order items
- Refunds
- Products
- Marketing campaigns

The analysis focuses on customer behavior, conversion performance, revenue generation, and product profitability.

## Business Questions

1. Which traffic source drives the highest number of orders?
2. Which campaign generates the highest revenue?
3. Which device type generates more orders?
4. Which traffic source has the highest conversion rate?
5. What is the net revenue after refunds?
6. Which product generates the highest profit?
7. Do returning customers have a higher purchase rate than new customers?
8. Which primary product generates the most add-on purchases?
9. Which product has the highest refund rate?
10. Who are the top 10 customers by revenue?
11. How long does it take users to place an order?
12. Which traffic source generates the most returning customers?
13. Which product pairs are most frequently purchased together?

## Key Findings

- Gsearch generated the highest number of orders and returning customers.
- The nonbrand campaign generated the highest revenue.
- Desktop users generated significantly more orders than mobile users.
- The Original Mr. Fuzzy was the most profitable product.
- Returning customers achieved a higher purchase rate than new customers.
- The company generated approximately $1.85 million in net revenue after refunds.
- The Original Mr. Fuzzy and The Hudson River Mini Bear were the most frequently purchased product pair.
